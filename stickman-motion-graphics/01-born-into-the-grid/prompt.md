# Prompt — 01-born-into-the-grid

The original prompt used to generate this motion graphic:

create a new directory, name this one like stickman-motion-graphics or something like that

then within there, create these 6 scenes (give each a name):

**Scene 1 — Born Into the Grid**
*Duration: 12 seconds*

> A white canvas. A vast grid of identical small colored boxes fills the entire frame — hundreds of them, edge to edge, each one a tiny house. They are all the same size, the same shape, the same pale yellow. A stickman is born into the center of the grid — a small circle appears first, then two arms, two legs, a body, drawn on in sequence like a child's sketch coming to life. He is blue. Every other stickman visible in the neighboring boxes is gray.
>
> The camera slowly pulls back — the grid expanding as it retreats — revealing that the grid is even larger than it first appeared. Thousands of boxes. The blue stickman's box is completely indistinguishable from every other box at this scale. He is one cell in an enormous organism. He looks left. He looks right. The stickmen in adjacent boxes are doing exactly what he is doing, in perfect synchrony. He pauses. They don't.

**Scene 2 — The Conveyor Belt**
*Duration: 15 seconds*

> A long horizontal conveyor belt runs across the full width of the canvas at vertical center. On it, a crowd of gray stickmen ride from left to right — packed together, evenly spaced, all facing right, all moving at the same speed. They don't walk. The belt carries them. Their legs hang loose.
>
> Our blue stickman enters from the left and steps onto the belt with them. He rides. He looks around at the others. Nobody makes eye contact. After a moment he starts walking — actually walking, legs animating — trying to move faster than the belt. He gains a few positions on the gray stickmen around him. Then the belt speeds up. He is back where he started relative to the crowd. He walks faster. The belt speeds up again. His legs animate faster, almost frantic, and he stays exactly in place within the crowd.
>
> He stops walking. The belt carries him. He looks at his feet. A label fades in above the belt in small gray type: *"productivity."* It fades out.

---

**Scene 3 — The Ladder**
*Duration: 18 seconds*

> A single tall ladder dominates the canvas — extending from below the bottom edge to above the top edge, so neither the ground nor the ceiling is visible. It occupies the left third of the frame. On every rung, a gray stickman stands, facing upward, waiting. The rungs are packed. There is barely space between figures.
>
> Our blue stickman enters at the bottom and begins climbing. He passes gray stickman after gray stickman. The climb is slow — each rung takes effort, his body language showing it, arms pulling, legs stepping. As he climbs, the canvas scrolls upward with him, revealing more rungs above, always more figures ahead, the top never coming into view.
>
> Halfway up, he looks to the right side of the canvas — empty. No ladder there. Just open white space. A stickman on the right side of the canvas, red, is not climbing. He is building something — laying down flat platforms, constructing a structure horizontally outward rather than vertically upward. The blue stickman watches him for a long moment. Then looks back up at the ladder. The gray stickmen above him haven't moved.

---

**Scene 4 — The Room Where Decisions Are Made**
*Duration: 20 seconds*

> A large rectangular room, rendered in simple flat lines — four walls, a floor, a ceiling, all visible in a slight isometric perspective. A long table runs down the center. Around it sit twelve stickmen — all gray, all large, all seated. They have top hats. Small ones, barely noticeable, but there.
>
> Our blue stickman stands outside the room, visible through a gap in the wall. He is much smaller than the figures at the table — not because he is far away, but because the scale difference is deliberate. He watches. The figures at the table pass a single document around — it travels from stickman to stickman, each one making a mark on it.
>
> The document reaches the edge of the table closest to the blue stickman. It slides off the table and floats toward him through the gap in the wall. He catches it. He looks at it. On it is drawn a small stickman — blue — with an arrow pointing downward. He looks up at the table. The twelve gray figures are already looking at something else. The document was never about him. He just receives what it says.

---

**Scene 5 — The Crowd Moves One Way**
*Duration: 18 seconds*

> A massive crowd of gray stickmen fills the canvas completely, all walking in the same direction — left to right, a steady human current. They move at the same pace. The canvas is dense with figures, shoulder to shoulder, a river of identical movement.
>
> Our blue stickman is somewhere in the middle of this crowd, being carried along by it. He isn't walking — the crowd's momentum moves him. He tries to stop — his legs plant, his body leans back — but the crowd presses him forward. He tries to turn around — faces left, tries to walk against the current. The gray stickmen don't react, don't part, don't notice him. They simply flow around him and through him like water around a stone, and he is slowly rotated back to face right by the sheer pressure of bodies.
>
> He stops resisting. He faces right again. He moves with the crowd. The camera slowly pulls back to reveal where the crowd is heading — a large door at the right edge of the canvas. Above the door, in small plain type, a single word. The crowd files through without slowing. The blue stickman reaches the door. He pauses at the threshold. He is the only one who pauses. Then he goes through.

---

**Scene 6 — The Gap**
*Duration: 20 seconds*

> Two platforms float in the center of a white canvas — one on the left, one on the right, separated by a gap. The left platform is low. The right platform is high — significantly higher, the difference unmistakable. On the right platform, a cluster of gray stickmen stand comfortably, talking to each other, gesturing, animated and at ease. On the left platform, our blue stickman stands alone, looking across the gap.
>
> The gap is not enormous. It looks crossable. He backs up, runs, and jumps. He almost makes it — his hands reach the edge of the right platform. A gray stickman on the right platform looks down at him. Pauses. Then steps back from the edge. Blue stickman falls. He lands back on the left platform, bounces once, settles.
>
> He looks at the gap again. It is the same distance. But now he notices something — the right platform is very slowly rising. Barely perceptible. Over the remaining duration of the scene it moves upward by 20% of its original height. The gap is not just horizontal. It is growing vertically too. The stickmen on the right platform haven't noticed. They are still talking.
