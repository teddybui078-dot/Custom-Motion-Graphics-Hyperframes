# Prompt — 30-money-tree

The original prompt used to generate this motion graphic:

Duration: 20 seconds — Green & White

White canvas. Complete stillness for one full second. Then the bank building drops in from above — flat illustrated style, bold green fill #1D9E75, white windows, clean geometric shapes. It falls from off-canvas carrying 3 frames of vertical motion blur, landing at dead center with a full cinematic hit: 2-frame white overexposure flash, radial shockwave ring expanding from its base outward to canvas edges in 8 frames, canvas snapping 6px downward and correcting on the next frame, the building compressing 14% vertically and stretching 8% horizontally on impact, RGB channels separating 4px for 3 frames. The building bounces — overshooting its resting scale by 10%, oscillating through two decreasing bounces before settling. It owns the center of the frame. It is not going anywhere.
Seconds 2–5: Money arrives.
From the left edge of the canvas a thick pressurized stream of money launches toward the bank — dense, fast, a river of chunky green bills and coins tumbling over each other, carrying heavy motion blur. A second stream enters simultaneously from the right edge, then a third from the top. Three rivers of money converging on the bank from three directions simultaneously, each stream hitting the building on the same frame. Triple cinematic impact: the white flash is three times brighter, holding for 3 frames instead of 2. Three shockwave rings launch simultaneously from three points of contact, overlapping as they expand, creating an interference pattern across the canvas. The bank compresses in all three impact directions at once — a full-body squash, the building momentarily unrecognizable before springing back to its original shape through two full oscillations. The canvas snaps 5px in three directions across three frames. Chromatic aberration fires hard — 5px channel separation for 4 frames. The building absorbs all three streams. The impacts settle. The canvas recovers.
The bank pulses — a deep green radial glow expanding from its walls, slow, deliberate, the glow of something that has just fed. It pulses twice. On the second pulse it is visibly larger than when it landed — it has grown 8% from absorbing the money, the scale increase subtle but real. A bold white dollar sign appears centered on the building face, springing in with a single overshoot bounce, settling. The bank is capitalized.
Seconds 5–10: The tree branches out.
Eight branch lines erupt simultaneously from the bank — thick 5px green strokes, four arcing upward and four arcing downward, spreading across the canvas like roots or lightning, each line finding its own path outward. They draw themselves fast, the stroke tip traveling from bank to canvas edge with a hard ease-out, the lines slightly irregular — not perfect arcs, but organic curves that feel grown rather than drawn. The branching happens in 1.5 seconds, the eight lines filling the canvas with a dense green network radiating from center.
As each branch line reaches its endpoint, an investor node detonates into existence — a rounded square badge, 72px, white fill, bold green border 2px, a small icon and one-word label inside. Each arrival is a miniature cinematic hit: the node drops in from 16px above its resting position carrying motion blur, lands with a white flash lasting 1 frame, a small shockwave ring expanding from its badge edge, the badge compressing and springing back through two bounces. The bevel highlight on each badge flashes to 60% opacity on impact then settles to resting state. The eight nodes land in a tight cascade — each one 6 frames after the previous, the arrivals rolling outward from the bank like a wave, the last node landing exactly as the first has fully settled.
The canvas is now fully populated — bank at center, eight glowing green-bordered investor nodes at the ends of eight radiating branches, the white canvas filled with the diagram of distribution.
Seconds 10–14: Money routes to investors.
Money packets launch simultaneously from the bank along all eight branches — chunky green rectangles, bold, carrying directional motion blur, traveling the branch lines like bullets fired down tracks. They move fast. Each packet hits its investor node with a cinematic impact: 1-frame white flash, small shockwave ring, node compressing and springing back, bevel highlight spiking. The eight impacts don't land simultaneously — they land in sequence as each packet completes its journey, the packets on shorter branches arriving first, longer branches arriving later. The staggered impacts create a rolling percussion across the canvas — eight hits, each one clean and physical, the canvas absorbing each one and recovering before the next. After each impact the investor node glows briefly — a green pulse radiating from its badge — then settles, now holding a small green number in its corner representing the capital received.
Seconds 14–18: The loans go out and the interest comes back.
From four of the eight investor nodes — the four largest, their badges now slightly bigger than the others from the capital they received — new lines grow back toward the bank. These return lines are thinner than the originals, 2px, dashed, animated — the dashes traveling along the line toward the bank continuously, like a flow indicator showing direction of movement. They grow from investor to bank over 1 second, all four simultaneously, thin green dashed lines threading back through the existing branch network toward center.
Along these return lines, money packets travel back toward the bank. But these packets are visibly larger than the ones that were sent out — fatter, more saturated green, carrying more motion blur because they are moving with more urgency. The interest is physically visible in the size difference. Each return packet travels its dashed line and hits the bank with a full cinematic impact — white flash, shockwave ring, canvas snap, chromatic aberration. The bank grows 3% in scale each time a return payment lands, the growth immediate and physical, like something being inflated. Four return hits across 3 seconds — the bank visibly larger after each one, accumulating mass, the white dollar sign on its face scaling up proportionally with each hit.
Above the bank, after the second return hit, a large bold percentage sign springs in — bright green, 96px, SF Pro Display Heavy — with its own full spring bounce entrance, overshooting and settling. It reads: +12%. It hangs above the building like a flag, pulsing gently in sync with each subsequent return payment landing below it.
Seconds 18–20: The full picture holds.
All motion stops simultaneously on second 18. The canvas freezes — bank at center, now noticeably larger than its starting size, the percentage sign above it, eight branch lines radiating outward, eight investor nodes at their endpoints, four dashed return lines threading back to center. The entire diagram visible and legible. The system exposed.
The camera begins a slow pull back — the entire diagram shrinking toward center over 2 seconds, the white canvas expanding around it, revealing that the diagram occupies only the center third of the frame at this scale. Around it, the rest of the canvas is empty white. The bank and its network are smaller than they seemed up close.
On the final frame, one line of bold green text fades in at the bottom of the canvas — centered, 32px, tracked wide. It is the only text in the entire scene. It holds as the animation ends.
