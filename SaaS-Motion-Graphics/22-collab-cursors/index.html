Design Principles for Calm Software

Good software feels calm. It respects attention, moves with intention, and never asks for more focus than a task truly deserves.

Clarity over cleverness

Clarity means saying less. The interface should never make a reader work to understand what just changed, or why it matters on the page in front of them.

When everything competes for attention, nothing earns it. Restraint is the feature that makes every other feature legible.

Motion with meaning

Every animation should explain a change — where something came from, where it went, and how the parts of the product relate to one another over time.
