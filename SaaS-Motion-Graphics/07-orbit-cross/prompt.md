# Prompt — 07-orbit-cross

The original prompt used to generate this motion graphic:

Black canvas. A single white sphere — rendered as a circle with a subtle radial gradient from white center to light gray edge, 48px radius — sits in the upper left third of the canvas, slightly off center. It is the only thing in the frame.
Very slowly, almost imperceptibly, it begins to move. Not in a straight line — in a long, wide arc, traveling across the canvas as if in orbit around a gravitational point outside the frame. It moves at a pace that makes you question whether it's moving at all for the first two seconds.
As it passes through the center third of the canvas, a faint trail materializes behind it — not a line, but a series of progressively more transparent copies of itself, each slightly smaller, like a comet tail rendered in still frames. The trail fades completely by the time the sphere exits the right side of canvas.
A second sphere enters from the bottom left — same size, slightly warmer color. It follows a different arc, crossing the path left by the first. Where the two paths would have intersected, a small node pulses once — a single ring expansion — and disappears. The second sphere exits. The canvas returns to black. Then one line of type fades in where the intersection was. Holds. Fades out.
