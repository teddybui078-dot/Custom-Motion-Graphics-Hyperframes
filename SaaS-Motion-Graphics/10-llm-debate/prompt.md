# Prompt — 10-llm-debate

The original prompt used to generate this motion graphic:

Dan & John — The LLM Debate (Bevel & Emboss)

Dark canvas #1C1C1E, 1920×1080, 20 seconds. Full-frame Messages conversation, no device chrome. Background fixed. Messages scroll upward as the conversation fills the frame.
Surface treatment — Bevel & Emboss on every bubble: Each message bubble carries a subtle bevel. A 1px highlight stroke sits along the top and left edges of every bubble — white at 18% opacity. A 1px shadow stroke sits along the bottom and right edges — pure black at 25% opacity. The interior of each bubble has an extremely faint inner glow: white at 8% opacity for the white bubbles, a lighter blue at 12% opacity for the blue bubbles, radiating inward from all edges and fading to nothing by 8px in. The combined effect makes every bubble feel physically pressed — like a soft rubber key, slightly raised from the surface of the canvas. Not glossy, not flat. Tactile.
The typing indicator bubble carries the same bevel. The reaction badges carry a miniature version — the highlight and shadow strokes scaled down to 0.5px to match their smaller footprint.
The sender name labels and timestamps sit flat — no bevel, no treatment. Only the bubbles are embossed.
Typography: SF Pro, 17px, line-height 1.4. Bubble corner radius 18px with the sharp corner on the sender's side. Dan sends in white bubbles right-aligned. John sends in #2C7BE5 blue bubbles left-aligned. Sender name in 12px gray on first message only. Timestamps in centered 12px gray every 4–5 messages.
Entrance physics: Every bubble drops in from 12px above its resting position, overshoots 4px below, returns and settles — spring bounce, not easing. As the bubble lands, the bevel highlight briefly intensifies — white edge strokes flashing to 35% opacity for 3 frames before settling back to 18% — as if the bubble is pressing into a surface on impact. The bounce and the bevel flash happen together, making the arrival feel physical and weighty.
Typing indicators — three animated dots in a beveled bubble — appear 0.5 seconds before each message, then snap-replace with the actual bubble.
