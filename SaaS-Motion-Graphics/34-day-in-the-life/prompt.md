# Prompt — 34-day-in-the-life

The original prompt used to generate this motion graphic:

The Day in the Life Ad
Duration: 30 seconds

White canvas. A clock icon appears at the top — 9:00 AM. A product dashboard fills the frame beneath it. A cursor enters and within 4 seconds completes a morning standup check: reads team activity from yesterday, sees three tasks flagged for today, sends one quick comment on a blocked task. Everything takes 4 seconds of screen time. The clock advances to 11:00 AM. Hard cut.
11:00 AM. A client report needs to go out. The cursor clicks Export, selects a template, the report generates in 2 seconds — a PDF preview sliding in from the right, fully formatted, branded, ready. The cursor clicks Send. An email confirmation toast appears. The cursor moves away. 6 seconds of screen time. Clock advances to 2:00 PM. Hard cut.
2:00 PM. A new team member's avatar appears in the sidebar — just joined. The cursor clicks their profile and assigns three starter tasks with two clicks each. The new member's task list populates in real time on a split-screen right panel. 5 seconds. Clock advances to 5:00 PM. Hard cut.
5:00 PM. The cursor opens a weekly summary panel. Three metrics count up simultaneously — tasks completed, time saved, goals hit. All three land on green values. The cursor closes the laptop — the browser tab closes, the white canvas remains, the clock shows 5:00 PM.
A headline fades in centered: "A full day's work, actually finished." The product logo appears below it. A CTA button springs in. Holds.
