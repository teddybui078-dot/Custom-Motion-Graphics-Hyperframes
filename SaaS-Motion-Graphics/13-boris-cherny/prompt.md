# Prompt — 13-boris-cherny

Take the complete Boris Cherny Apple Text Animation prompt and add these two treatments across every text element. Nothing else changes.
Bevel & Emboss on all text:
Every word carries a subtle bevel — not on a bubble or container, directly on the letterforms themselves. A 1px white highlight sits along the top edge of each letter stroke at 20% opacity. A 1px dark shadow sits along the bottom edge of each letter stroke at 28% opacity. An extremely faint inner glow radiates from within each letterform — white at 10% opacity on dark text, fading to nothing by 3px inward. The combined effect makes every word feel pressed into the canvas from behind, slightly three-dimensional, the way Apple's display typography catches light on a physical product surface. Heavy weight words at 72px and above carry a stronger bevel — highlight at 28%, shadow at 36% — because the larger letterforms have more surface area to catch light. Tertiary lines at 28px carry a softer version — highlight at 12%, shadow at 18% — so they don't compete with the headlines.
Word-by-word with bevel flash on arrival:
Every phrase — including ones previously described as fading in whole — now builds strictly word by word. Each word materializes individually: opacity 0 to 1, scale 0.96 to 1.0, ease-out cubic over 18 frames. The stagger between words is 10 frames — slightly slower than before, giving each word a moment to assert itself before the next arrives. As each word lands at full scale, the bevel highlight flashes — jumping from its resting opacity to 55% for 3 frames, then easing back down over 8 frames. The flash is a brief catch of light, as if each word is being pressed into the canvas at the moment of arrival. Single heavy words like "Engineer." and "Claude Code." get a stronger flash — highlight hitting 70% for 4 frames — because their weight demands it.
Exit bevel:
On exit, the bevel reverses — the shadow strokes briefly intensify to 50% opacity for 3 frames as the word begins to leave, as if lifting off the surface creates a shadow before the word disappears. Then the word exits normally — scale 1.0 to 1.04, opacity 1 to 0 over 14 frames.
"Boris Cherny" final line:
The closing name carries the lightest bevel of the entire sequence — highlight at 10%, shadow at 14%, no flash on arrival. It materializes softly, without the landing impact of everything before it. After all the weight and flash of the preceding words, his name arrives quietly. The bevel is there but it whispers.
