# Prompt — 12-cursor-onboarding

The original prompt used to generate this motion graphic:

A white canvas. A single cursor arrow — the standard OS pointer, rendered in crisp black, no embellishment — appears at the top left of the canvas. It is the only thing in the frame.
It moves. Not on a straight path — it moves the way a real person moves a cursor, with slight irregularities, micro-corrections, the occasional small overshoot before landing on a target. It travels toward the center of the canvas where nothing yet exists.
As the cursor approaches center, a sign-up form materializes beneath it — fading in just in time for the cursor to arrive, as if the UI is being rendered on demand for this specific user. The form is complete and beautiful — name field, email field, password field, a single CTA button.
The cursor clicks into the name field. A text input cursor appears and a name types itself — fast, the way a real person types their own name from muscle memory. Tab to the next field. Email types in. Tab. Password types in, masked. The cursor travels to the CTA button — that slight human drift and correction — and clicks.
The form dissolves. In its place, a complete product dashboard fades in — populated, personalized, ready. The cursor moves to the center of the dashboard and stops. It sits there for two full seconds, the way a person sits when they first see something that works exactly as they hoped. Then the cursor fades out. The product remains. It doesn't need the user anymore — it's alive on its own.
