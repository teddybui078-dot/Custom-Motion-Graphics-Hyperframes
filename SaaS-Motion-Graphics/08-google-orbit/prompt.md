# Prompt — 08-google-orbit

The original prompt used to generate this motion graphic:

A white canvas, 1920×1080. The Google logo mark sits at the exact center of the frame — the four-color G, rendered crisp and large at 96px, static, never moving. It is the gravitational anchor of everything that follows. It appears first, fading in cleanly over 12 frames from opacity 0 to 1 with a subtle scale from 0.94 to 1.0, ease-out cubic. Once it lands it never moves again.
Six orbital paths exist around it — perfect circles, all centered on the Google G. They are not all the same radius. Three distinct orbital rings: the innermost at radius 160px, the middle at 240px, the outermost at 320px. Two products share each ring, positioned opposite each other — 180° apart — so the composition is always balanced. The orbital paths themselves are rendered as 0.5px strokes, color #E8E8E8, extremely faint. They are structural, not decorative. They appear simultaneously with the G, fading in at the same time.
The six product icons — Google Drive, Google Docs, Google Slides, Chrome, Gemini, Google Sites — each live in a white circular badge: 56×56px circle, white fill, 0.5px border in #E0E0E0, a soft shadow that is barely perceptible. Each badge holds the product's official icon centered at 28px. They begin off-canvas, invisible.
The icons enter one at a time, staggered by 14 frames each, in clockwise order starting from the 12 o'clock position of the innermost ring. Each icon springs into its orbital position — scaling from 0 to 1.05 to 1.0 with a single overshoot, settling in 10 frames. As each icon lands, a thin connector line draws from the edge of the Google G outward to the icon's badge — 0.5px, color #4285F4 Google blue, drawing itself in 8 frames with ease-out. The line does not follow the orbital path — it is a straight radial spoke, direct center to icon.
Once all six icons have landed and all six spokes are drawn, the orbital animation begins. All six icons begin traveling their respective circular paths simultaneously — each moving clockwise at a slightly different angular velocity so no two icons ever align. The innermost ring moves fastest, the outermost slowest. The velocity difference is subtle — not dramatic, just enough that the orbits feel independent rather than locked. The spoke lines rotate with each icon, always pointing radially back to center, so the G is always connected to all six products by six blue lines that sweep the canvas as the icons orbit.
The badges do not rotate as they travel — they remain upright at all times, the product icon inside always facing the same direction regardless of orbital position.
As each icon passes the 12 o'clock position of its orbit, its badge briefly brightens — a 4-frame opacity lift from 1.0 to 1.0 with a 1.06 scale pulse, then back — like a highlight passing over it. Subtle. A clock-tick of acknowledgment.
The overall color palette is restrained: white canvas, near-white badges, #E8E8E8 orbital tracks, #4285F4 blue spokes. The only color in the frame comes from the Google G at center and the product icons inside the badges. The blue spokes are the most visually active element — six lines rotating around a fixed center, sweeping the white canvas continuously.
The animation loops seamlessly. The loop point is when all six icons return to their starting positions — the moment just before the first icon springs in, cut back to the fully-orbiting state so the loop is invisible in motion.
