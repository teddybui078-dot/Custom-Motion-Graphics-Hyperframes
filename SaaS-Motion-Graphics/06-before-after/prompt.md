# Prompt — 06-before-after

The original prompt used to generate this motion graphic:

A white canvas. A single vertical line draws itself from the top of the canvas to the bottom, splitting it cleanly in half. The line draws at a steady pace — not fast, not slow. It feels like a referee drawing a boundary.
On the left side, three metric rows build in one at a time. Each row is a label and a progress bar. The label fades in first, then the bar grows from zero to its full width — and these are long bars, filling most of the available space, colored a muted red. They feel heavy. All three build with the same rhythm, staggered slightly.
On the right side, the same three rows build in the same way. But the bars are short — dramatically shorter — and green. They feel resolved. The contrast with the left side is immediate and stark.
Once both sides are fully built, the right-side numbers animate — counting downward from the left-side values to the right-side values simultaneously. The count eases out so it decelerates as it approaches the final number, landing cleanly.
Below the right-side metrics, a checkmark draws itself with a single continuous stroke — two lines meeting at a point, drawn in sequence like a hand writing a check. It settles. The scene holds.
