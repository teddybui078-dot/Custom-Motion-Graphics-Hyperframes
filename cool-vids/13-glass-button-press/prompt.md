# Prompt — 13-glass-button-press

The original prompt used to generate this motion graphic:

Glass Button Press
Duration: 10 seconds

A single scene, single element. A dark canvas with a dense colorful background — rich gradients, abstract shapes, vivid colors filling every inch of the frame behind the glass. The background is deliberately busy to show the glass working.
A single glass button sits dead center. Large — 280px wide, 72px tall, border-radius 36px. Frosted glass surface: backdrop blur 20px, white tint 15%, inset highlight stroke top and left edges at 55% white opacity, drop shadow below at 30% black opacity. The button label reads one short phrase in white semi-transparent text, weight 500.
The button enters with a single spring pop — scale 0 to 1.06 to 1.0 over 14 frames. It holds for 2 seconds. The background behind it is visible through the frosted surface — blurred, shifted in color slightly, the glass distorting what's behind it.
A cursor enters and hovers over the button. The button scales up to 1.03 — a gentle hover lift. The inset highlight brightens to 65% opacity. The cursor clicks. The button compresses — scale drops to 0.95 on the click frame, then springs back through 1.04 to 1.0 over 10 frames. On the compression frame, the inset highlight flashes to 80% opacity and the drop shadow deepens. Then on the spring back, the surface brightens momentarily — a glass catching light as pressure releases. The button holds after the press, the background still visible through it.
