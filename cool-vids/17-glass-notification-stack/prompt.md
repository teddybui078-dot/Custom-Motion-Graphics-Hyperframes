# Prompt — 17-glass-notification-stack

The original prompt used to generate this motion graphic:

Glass Notification Stack
Duration: 14 seconds
A dark canvas — deep charcoal to black gradient, #111118 to #0a0a0f. Two enormous soft blobs drift slowly behind everything — a warm amber bloom upper right, a cool violet bloom lower left, their edges meeting at canvas center and creating a shifting color temperature across the frame. The background is never static.
A phone screen occupies the center two thirds of the canvas — no device chrome, just the screen surface, its edges implied by a very faint vignette. A blurred home screen is visible — soft app icon shapes, a wallpaper beneath them, everything out of focus. This is the lock screen layer. The glass notifications will sit above it.
The first notification slides in from the top edge — a wide glass pill, full screen width minus 24px margins, 76px tall, border-radius 22px. It enters fast and decelerates sharply, spring-settling into the upper third of the screen. Frosted glass surface: backdrop blur 18px, white tint 10%, inset rim highlight top edge at 45% opacity. Inside it, an app icon materializes on the left — a small 32px rounded square, also glassy. An app name fades in at 11px gray. A notification headline appears beneath it at 14px white weight 500, typing itself character by character at a natural pace. A timestamp fades in at the right edge.
The notification holds for 2 seconds. A second notification enters from the top — same spring entrance, slightly narrower, settling 12px below and behind the first, creating a stack. Its content types in. A third notification enters — stacking behind the second. The three cards form a perspective stack — each successive card slightly smaller and lower, the frosted surfaces of all three visible simultaneously, each one showing the blurred home screen through its glass at a slightly different position.
The cursor appears and swipes upward on the stack. The cards fan out — separating from the stacked perspective into a flat vertical list, each card springing to its own row with individual bounce animations. The stack becomes a list. All three cards now full width, full height, separated by 10px gaps. The cursor taps the second card. It expands — growing downward to reveal more content inside the glass surface. Holds. Then collapses back.
