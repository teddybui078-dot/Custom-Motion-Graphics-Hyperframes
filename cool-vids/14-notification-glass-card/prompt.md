# Prompt — 14-notification-glass-card

The original prompt used to generate this motion graphic:

Notification Glass Card
Duration: 12 seconds

A phone screen in portrait — dark background visible, a home screen of soft app icons blurred behind a glass notification card. The background is rich and colorful — enough visual information behind the glass to show the frosting effect clearly.
The notification card enters from the top of the phone screen, sliding down from off-canvas with a spring ease — overshooting 8px, settling into position in the upper third of the screen. It is a wide glass pill — full width minus 20px margins, 80px tall, border-radius 20px. Frosted glass surface with a white tint, inset rim highlight, and a soft shadow pulling it off the background.
Inside the notification: an app icon on the left materializes — a small rounded square, also glassy. An app name fades in beside it in small text. A notification headline types itself in below — one line, white, weight 500, appearing character by character at a natural reading pace. A timestamp fades in on the right edge.
The notification holds for 3 seconds. Then a second notification card enters from the top, pushing the first one down — both now visible, stacked with a 12px gap, the stack slightly compressed to fit. The new notification types its headline in. Both cards hold. The background is visible through both frosted surfaces simultaneously — the same blurred app icons showing through both at slightly different positions due to their different vertical placement.
