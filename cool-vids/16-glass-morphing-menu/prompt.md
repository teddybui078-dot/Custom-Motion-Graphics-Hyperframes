# Prompt — 16-glass-morphing-menu

The original prompt used to generate this motion graphic:

Glass Morphing Menu
Duration: 14 seconds

A dark canvas with a vivid background — a landscape or abstract scene, colorful and detailed, providing maximum contrast for the glass effect. A glass pill sits at the bottom center — a navigation bar, four icon slots, frosted and glowing with the background colors bleeding through its surface.
A cursor taps the center icon. The navigation pill morphs — it expands upward and outward, the border-radius holding at the pill level as the shape grows, transforming from a small bottom nav pill into a full context menu card over 20 frames. The morph is a single continuous spring — the glass surface expanding like liquid filling a mold, the frosted area growing to cover more of the background, more of the background color showing through the larger glass surface as it expands.
Inside the expanded glass card, menu items fade in one at a time top to bottom — each item a row with an icon and a label, appearing with a 6-frame stagger. Four items total. The glass surface of the expanded card catches the full range of background colors now — the larger surface area revealing more of the scene behind it.
A cursor clicks one menu item. The card morphs back — contracting back to the original pill shape over 16 frames, the spring pulling it inward. The menu items fade out as the surface shrinks. The pill holds at the bottom, smaller, the background visible through it again. The morph felt liquid.
