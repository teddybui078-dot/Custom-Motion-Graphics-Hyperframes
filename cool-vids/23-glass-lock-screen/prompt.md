# Prompt — 23-glass-lock-screen

The original prompt used to generate this motion graphic:

Glass Lock Screen
Duration: 20 seconds

A full-canvas phone screen — portrait 9:16. A rich wallpaper fills the entire frame — an abstract colorful scene, vivid and detailed, slightly animated with slow color shifts throughout the duration. This is what the glass will work against.
The lock screen assembles itself from top to bottom. A glass status bar materializes at the top — full width, 44px tall, border-radius 0, containing time on the left and system icons on the right. Pure frosted glass, barely there, just a tint and a rim highlight along the bottom edge.
The time display materializes at canvas center — enormous, SF Pro Display Heavy, white, the hour and minute digits each appearing with a fast scale-in. The date fades in below it in smaller gray text.
A glass notification appears in the lower half — the same pill design from Scene 1. Then a second. Then a third. They stack with the perspective treatment.
At the very bottom, a glass home indicator bar materializes — a short wide pill, frosted, the standard iPhone home bar. Above it, a camera icon glass pill on the left and a flashlight glass pill on the right — both appearing with spring pops.
A finger swipe gesture travels upward from the home bar. The entire lock screen lifts — translating upward, accelerating, all glass elements moving together as one surface being pulled away. The wallpaper stays. The lock screen glass elements travel upward off the canvas, revealing the home screen beneath. The last element to leave is the time display — the largest, the heaviest, exiting last, its white numerals the final thing visible before the screen is clear.
