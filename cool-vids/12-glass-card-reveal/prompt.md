# Prompt — 12-glass-card-reveal

The original prompt used to generate this motion graphic:

Glass Card Reveal
Duration: 15 seconds
A dark canvas — a deep purple-navy gradient filling the frame edge to edge. Two enormous soft color blobs drift slowly in the background — one warm coral upper right, one cool teal lower left — their edges bleeding into each other at the center of the canvas, creating a shifting color landscape that changes the tint of everything above it.
Three glass cards materialize in a horizontal row, center frame. They don't appear simultaneously — they rise from 20px below their resting positions one at a time, left to right, each with a spring bounce. Each card is a rounded rectangle — backdrop blur 16px, white tint 10% opacity, inset highlight stroke creating a physical top-left rim, a soft drop shadow below. The cards are slightly different sizes — the center card taller than the two flanking it, elevated by 12px.
As each card settles, its content fades in inside it — an icon materializing first, then a headline word by word, then a descriptor line. The glass surface of each card reflects the background blobs differently — the left card catching more of the coral warmth, the right card tinted cooler by the teal bloom, the center card sitting at the convergence point of both, its glass surface a neutral frosted white.
Once all three cards are fully revealed, the background blobs accelerate slightly — drifting faster, the colors shifting beneath the glass surfaces. The cards' tints shift in real time with the blobs moving beneath them. The glass is alive with what's behind it.
