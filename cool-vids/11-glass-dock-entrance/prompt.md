# Prompt — 11-glass-dock-entrance

The original prompt used to generate this motion graphic:

Glass Dock Entrance
Duration: 12 seconds
A rich dark canvas — deep navy to purple gradient, #1a1a2e to #533483. Three large soft color blobs sit in the background — a purple bloom upper left, a teal bloom lower right, a pink bloom center right — all blurred and slow-drifting, moving imperceptibly across the scene over the full duration.
The dock enters from below the canvas bottom edge. It rises upward with a spring — overshooting its resting position by 12px, bouncing back through two oscillations before settling at vertical center. The dock is a frosted glass pill — backdrop blur heavy, a white tint at 12% opacity, an inset highlight stroke along the top and left edges at 50% white opacity creating a physical rim of light.
Six app icon slots inside the dock are invisible at the start. They materialize one at a time left to right — each icon scaling from 0 to 1.08 to 1.0 with a spring pop, 8 frames between each. As each icon lands its glass surface catches the nearest background blob's color — a faint tint of that blob bleeding through the frosted surface at the icon's position.
Once all six icons are placed, a cursor enters from the right and hovers over the third icon. The icon scales up to 1.2 and lifts — its glass surface brightening, the inset highlight intensifying. The adjacent icons scale up slightly in sympathy — 1.08 — creating the macOS dock magnification effect. The cursor moves away. All icons settle back. The dock holds, the background blobs still drifting behind it.
